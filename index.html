Identity / System — 2024

LSE
CATERING

A visual system developed for LSE Catering, addressing the need for clarity across a complex and high-density environment.

Project 01 / 07

The project focuses on communication under pressure — menus, signage, and information that must be immediately understood, often in transitional spaces. The approach begins with structure: a consistent grid, clear typographic hierarchy, and a reduced set of visual elements.

Rather than introducing a new visual language, the system works through refinement. Existing elements are reorganised and simplified, allowing information to emerge with greater clarity and consistency.

Typography becomes the primary tool. Scale, spacing and alignment are used to guide reading behaviour, reducing friction in moments of decision — queuing, ordering, moving through space.

The system is designed to extend across formats: printed menus, digital displays, and environmental signage. Each application follows the same underlying logic, ensuring continuity without repetition. The result is a quieter form of identity — one that operates through use rather than visibility.

Textile / Collection — Italy

FONT
MARINE &
BOTANICAL

A textile study that codifies the organic irregularities of the sea and shoreline into a precise visual identity.

Project 02 / 07

The collection functions as a translation of movement. The brief required a system that captures the life of the sea without falling into literal representation. The response was to develop a series of high-contrast motifs that mimic the ebb and flow of tides and the rhythmic structure of coral and flora.

Rather than static prints, the designs are engineered to interact with the garment's architecture. Sinuous, wave-like lines (Teal/Black) and explosive botanical forms (Red/White) are scaled to accentuate the body's motion, ensuring the pattern's logic remains intact from the tension of swimwear to the fluid drape of the maxi-silhouette.

The palette — chromatic teals, deep corals, and stark monochromes — is used as a navigational tool to distinguish between the various depths of the collection. The result is a refined textile language where nature is not just a motif, but a functional framework for the design.

Photography / Interiors — London

ESTATE
PHOTOGRAPHY

Architectural and interior photography for residential spaces in London — capturing the tension between raw structure and considered domestic life.

Project 03 / 07

Estate photography occupies a particular and underrated position in visual communication. It is not simply documentation. At its best, it is an argument — a case made in light, angle and moment for why a space deserves to be inhabited by the person looking at it.

This commission was for a converted Victorian warehouse apartment in London. The space is defined by its contradictions: exposed brick walls and steel I-beams sit alongside glass mezzanine balustrades and oak floors. The double-height living volume — with its original porthole window overlooking the Thames — demanded to be understood spatially, not just decoratively.

The photography works through patience and available light. No staging beyond the existing furniture, no artificial light sources. Each frame is chosen for its ability to communicate volume and materiality simultaneously — the way light breaks across brick, the relationship between a desk and a circular window, the view of St Paul's Cathedral that catches you without warning.

This is photography as a service that translates space into image — and into decision.

Record Artwork / Illustration — London

ABANA
SATIVA
JAZZ

Cover artwork for London-based DJ Abana — a hand-drawn visual world where jazz, smoke and flat colour collide into a single irreverent image.

Project 04 / 07

The brief was open: make something that feels like the record sounds. Abana's music sits at the intersection of jazz structure and loose, hazy energy — the kind of session that starts late and ends at dawn. The illustration had to hold that duality without explaining it.

The approach was deliberately naïve in technique but precise in composition. A single character — blocky, flat, expressionless — anchors both the front and back covers. On the front he plays, swallowed by his own instrument and the smoke that rises from it. On the back he turns away, the party over, the story told without words.

The palette is restricted to five colours: a deep cobalt blue, terra cotta, leaf green, mustard yellow and black on white. No gradients, no texture, no digital polish. The hand-drawn line is the only tool. Typography follows the same logic — loose, curved letterforms that feel drawn rather than set.

Catalogue reference BAS002. Artwork by Michele Bagnod. Written and produced by Sahil Dhingra. Mastered by Andreas Henneberg.

Artwork / Apparel — Ivrea, Italy

IVREATRONIC

Merch artwork for an independent record label rooted in Ivrea — the castle and the wilderness of the town compressed into a single gradient vision.

Project 05 / 07

Ivreatronic is an independent electronic music label born out of Ivrea, a small industrial city in the Piedmont region of northern Italy — best known for its medieval castle and the dense forests and hills that surround it. The brief asked for something fresh: not a historical reference, not a postcard, but something that felt like the music itself.

The artwork is built around two elements that define the town's physical reality: the castle, which rises above the skyline as the city's oldest symbol, and the wild, overgrown nature that presses in on all sides — foliage, branches, and the kind of vegetation that feels older than anything built around it. Both are rendered in silhouette, stripped of detail, and placed into a single compressed scene that reads like a fragment of a dream or a moment between day and night.

The gradient — running from a saturated hot magenta at the top through cobalt blue to a deeper twilight at the base — gives the image its energy. It references the visual language of rave-era graphics and synth-wave aesthetics without copying either directly. The result sits somewhere between poster art and digital illustration: bold enough to work on a black hoodie at scale, detailed enough to reward a closer look.

Applied across a limited merch collection — oversized t-shirts, hoodies — with the logotype set in a custom chromatic treatment that mirrors the gradient of the artwork.

Spatial Design / 3D Visualisation — Italy

SPATIAL
DESIGN

Interior renovation concept for a residential apartment — translating an existing space into a precise proposal through 3D visualisation.

Project 06 / 07

Spatial design begins before a single wall is touched. The challenge is not simply aesthetic — it is the problem of convincing a client, an owner, or a buyer that a space can be fundamentally different from what they are looking at. Visualisation is the tool that bridges that gap.

This project involved the renovation proposal for a residential apartment in Italy. The existing space was a layered accumulation of decades of habit — good bones, but cluttered form and an unclear spatial logic. The commission was to reimagine the apartment as a coherent, contemporary living space without expanding its footprint.

The proposal centralises a warm material palette around natural oak cabinetry, marble worktops and oak flooring — materials that unify kitchen, living and working areas into a single readable interior. A continuous low unit running the length of the main space doubles as kitchen island, sofa back, and writing desk, collapsing three programmes into one gesture.

The 3D renderings are produced in warm, ambient light to communicate atmosphere as well as layout — the goal is not a technical drawing but a promise of what daily life in the space could feel like.

Animation / Direction — 2016

YOUR
PLAYGROUND
IS THE SUN

Animated music video for Stefano Ritteri — story, drawing and editing by Michele Bagnod. Released on Congaloid.

Project 07 / 07

Animation offers something that most visual disciplines do not: time. Not just to show a thing, but to let it move, transform and accumulate meaning in sequence. This project was a complete single-author commission — from the initial concept and storyboard through to hand-drawn frames and final edit.

The brief from Stefano Ritteri was deliberately open. The track — warm, textured, deeply rhythmic — asked for something that felt handmade rather than produced. The response was an illustration-based animation drawn entirely by hand: a world of loose, organic mark-making that follows the music's own logic rather than trying to illustrate it literally.

The visual language is built on a small number of recurring motifs that shift and transform across the duration of the video — figures, light, landscape — treated as elements of a system rather than a narrative. The editing rhythm mirrors the track's structure: expansive passages of slow movement punctuated by moments of dense visual energy.

Released on Congaloid in December 2016. Story, drawing and editing by Michele Bagnod.
